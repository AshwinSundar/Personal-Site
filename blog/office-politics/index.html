Office Politics

Everyone has to play politics in an office. Not playing politics is a form of politics, too. When people say things like "I don't participate in office politics," that means they are refusing to participate in a set of informal rules, and abiding by their own set of what they believe to be internal rules for interacting with colleagues. The thing is, if enough people start to think like that, well that's the new "office politics" playground.

Another observation I've made is about people who claim to participate in office politics and drop "leads" in the form of what amounts to company gossip. Those people are generally perceived to be intelligent in a company. People who don't participate in office politics can also be seen as intelligent, if they are still openly aware of what the company is doing. The people who are unaware of office politics are generally seen as dumb. Interestingly, the people who are aware of office politics but do not let people know what they know are also seen as dumb.

But that last group of people retain the most power. Letting people know what you know is a way to rapidly dilute your knowledge power.

Of course, there's a balance to be had. Hiding from everyone and being vague is kinda pointless. Letting a few people know what you know, but keeping things relatively tight-lipped, is a good way to exchange a little bit of knowledge in order for some people to see you as intelligent. You do dilute your knowledge power a bit, but you exchange it for perceived intelligence. And people who are perceived to be intelligent will be approached by others and offered some of their knowledge power. It's like a mini Illuminati that lives within a company.

Arbitrage

Businesses are a form of arbitrage. Their goal is to expand their wealth. In theory, a business could operate as one person, working for free and manually processing raw materials into a product. But this isn't scalable. So businesses exchange the efficiency of this system for goods that will allow them to scale.

The arbitrageurs are those that sell business services, knowledge, or goods. Traditionally, we'd think of those who sell equipment and machinery as the arbitrageurs. But that's just one form. Another form is you and I. When you go to work for a company, you're engaging in arbitrage. You are exchanging your time and knowledge for a promise of a paycheck. It's a brilliant strategy by companies. They have you sign an NDA, which means they have a monopoly over your time and money. And in exchange they just PROMISE they'll pay you. They don't have to. They can stop paying you. They would take a hit in reputation and lose money long-term if people file lawsuits, but they can do that.

Illuminati

You don't get invited to be part of the Illuminati. The Illuminati just refers to anyone who has achieved a level of awareness about their world that lets them see the larger picture. I think there are a few baseline requirements to "become" Illuminati, or enlightened.

  1. You have to see how your immediate life feeds into the larger plan.
  2. You have to recognize that no matter what you think is the "larger" plan, there's always another plan that surrounds that plan. If you think that the company you work for is the master of your industry, you should probably be aware that the banks control your industry and actively make it so your company is at the top.
  3. You feed people bits and pieces of your thoughts, which dilutes your knowledge power in exchange for for other bits of knowledge. You only exchange this knowledge with other people who have something more valuable to exchange. You give them the impression that you've imparted something wise to them, and they'll be fooled into exchanging something far too valuable.

Number 3 is interesting. It's pretty much impossible to get away with money transfer frauds. If I offer to send you $100 worth of goods in exchange for a $100 bill and I don't do that, I can go to jail. If I offer to exchange you a worthless piece of information for anything, even money, then I'm pretty much fine. I could take a reputation hit if I'm not careful though.
