April 2022

Reddit is poison to the mind. It abolishes your ability to think freely, clearly, and most importantly, on your own terms. Instead, you are force-fed with the standard rhetoric of the highly-upvoted Reddit comment, which tends to be a semi-well articulated statement that is not really verifiable, but sounds pretty accurate. It's a highly-generalizable statement that uses probably the same set of 1000 words that every other top-rated Reddit comment uses, and the grammatical structure is extremely short and easy to digest. It's not really written like the last few sentences in other words.

But why is this poisonous? Well, it robs you of the your innate voice and your innate thinking patterns. We all think in unique ways, and arrive at realizations in our own amazing ways. So it's really sad to see that unique process abandoned for the common thought processes and group-think present in an online forum with a ranking system.

I don't think Reddit was always this way, along with most other popular internet forums. Something changed in the last 5-10 years that made these sites a lot more homogeneous in nature. It's almost as if everything is written by the same committee of people, who all agree on how to discuss issues and topics.

I'm so thoroughly bored with Reddit, but it somehow has wired itself into my addiction pathways deep inside my brain and I'm struggling to get it out. I know that spending more time with my hobbies, improving my education, and reading actual books is far healthier for me, but I still can't stop browsing Reddit insatiably during idle moments, or even when I'm feeling physically tired.

But I have to stop. It really is a poison to my mind, and I have to get it out before I just become another one of the sheep. Which is something I've spent my entire life trying to avoid. I don't want to waste all this effort I've made just because of a stupid website that I can't stop reading.

I want to be my own person. I've worked extremely hard at that my entire life, and nearly every decision I have made has been with that guiding philosophy as a cosmic background in my mind. I have no capability to think outside of that constraint, which frustrates me endlessly sometimes. I feel like other people don't have these types of mental constraints holding them back, because almost every single faculty of theirs at face value looks much more controlled than mine.
